# Days 1–5: Foundation and Conversion Baseline (Core)

Outcome:
Complete the minimum storefront foundation so a first-time Shopify merchant can present a clear offer, publish credible product pages, and establish trust elements before configuring advanced systems.

Steps:
1) Day 1 — Define a one-sentence offer promise and one primary customer segment.
2) Day 1 — Create a "must-have now" vs "defer" list to stop scope creep.
3) Day 2 — Build product information baseline: title, value-focused description, price logic, media, and clear delivery expectation.
4) Day 3 — Select theme based on speed/readability and apply only minimal branding (logo, colors, typography, navigation skeleton).
5) Day 4 — Add conversion blocks to product pages: core benefits, objections, guarantee/returns pointer, shipping expectation, and CTA clarity.
6) Day 5 — Publish trust stack: contact page, FAQ starter, policy pages, and basic about/brand credibility section.

Example:
A founder selling desk accessories keeps Day 1 scope to a single "organize your workspace in 5 minutes" promise, launches with one hero product, and defers homepage animations. By Day 5, product pages include shipping window + return policy links, reducing pre-purchase confusion.

Verification:
- Each product page answers: who it is for, why it is better, what it costs, when it arrives, and what happens if it is not a fit.
- Contact, shipping, and returns information are accessible within one click from product pages.
- Theme work is limited to functional essentials; no custom code is required for launch readiness.
