# Days 11–14: QA, Launch, and Week-1 Optimization (Evergreen)

Outcome:
Publish the store with controlled risk, validate tracking integrity, and execute a focused first-week marketing + optimization loop.

Steps:
1) Day 11 — Validate analytics and event tracking (product view, add to cart, checkout start, purchase) using test sessions.
2) Day 12 — Execute pre-launch QA across device/browser/payment/shipping scenarios and log pass/fail defects.
3) Day 13 — Follow launch-day runbook: backup key settings, publish, verify live checkout, and monitor first-hour behavior.
4) Day 14 — Start week-1 sequence: warm audience announcement, content-led traffic push, daily metric review, and issue triage.
5) Week 1 — Prioritize fixes by impact order: checkout blockers > trust gaps > product-page clarity > speed/performance improvements.

Example:
A founder catches a broken shipping rate on Day 12 during scenario testing, fixes profile assignment before launch, and avoids first-day checkout abandonment. During week 1, they observe high add-to-cart but low checkout start, then simplify shipping messaging on product pages.

Verification:
- Pre-launch QA log contains at least one full pass for mobile and desktop purchase paths.
- Launch-day checklist shows green status for domain, payments, shipping, taxes, and confirmation emails.
- Week-1 dashboard tracks sessions, add-to-cart rate, checkout start rate, purchase conversion, and top support questions.
