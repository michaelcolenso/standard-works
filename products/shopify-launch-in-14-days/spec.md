# Spec — Shopify Launch in 14 Days

## Product Overview
- **Working title:** Shopify Launch in 14 Days
- **Format:** 50–60 page PDF playbook + checklist bundle
- **Audience:** First-time Shopify store owners and side hustlers launching their first store
- **Core promise:** Remove launch overwhelm by giving a day-by-day execution path that prioritizes high-impact setup tasks and prevents common conversion-killing mistakes.

## Problem Statement
First-time Shopify merchants face analysis paralysis due to Shopify's large configuration surface (themes, settings, apps, policies, analytics, fulfillment options, and marketing channels). They frequently spend weeks on low-impact customization and neglect key trust, conversion, and tracking steps.

## Opportunity Signals
- “I’m overwhelmed and don’t know where to start.”
- “I need exact sequencing of tasks.”
- “I wasted time on the wrong setup work.”

These signals map to a strong demand for **prioritized sequence**, **decision support**, and **execution checklists** rather than broad theory.

## Target Buyer
### Primary Segment
- New Shopify merchants (0–1 launches completed)
- Side hustlers validating first ecommerce offer
- Budget-conscious DIY operators who cannot justify agency retainers

### Buyer Constraints
- Limited time (evenings/weekends)
- Limited technical confidence
- High fear of making irreversible setup mistakes

## Offer Design
### Core Deliverable
A guided 14-day launch sequence with explicit daily outputs:
1. Foundation and offer setup
2. Store structure and trust components
3. Product pages and conversion blocks
4. Shipping, tax, policies, and legal essentials
5. Payments and checkout optimization
6. App stack installation (must-have first)
7. Analytics and event tracking
8. Pre-launch QA and scenario testing
9. Launch day runbook
10. First 7-day post-launch optimization sprint

### Included Assets (MVP)
- Day-by-day timeline (14-day planner)
- Must-have vs nice-to-have decision matrix
- Theme customization checklist
- App recommendation table by use case
- Pre-launch testing protocol
- Post-launch week 1 marketing sequence
- Common mistakes + fixes reference

## Positioning
### Why this wins versus alternatives
- **Vs YouTube:** curated, ordered, and actionable instead of fragmented
- **Vs docs:** conversion-prioritized, not platform-generic
- **Vs courses:** lower price and faster implementation path
- **Vs agencies:** DIY affordability with clear execution support

## Pricing Hypothesis
- **Target launch price:** $67 (intro)
- **Price range testing:** $47–$97
- **Potential order bump (later phase):** Notion checklist tracker + editable launch board

## Distribution Channels
- Reddit: r/shopify, r/ecommerce, r/smallbusiness, r/sidehustle
- Shopify Community forums
- Founder-led content snippets (launch mistakes, day-by-day previews)

## MVP Scope and Constraints
### In-scope
- One high-quality PDF playbook (50–60 pages)
- 5–7 practical checklists/templates
- Launch and first-week post-launch playbooks

### Out-of-scope (v1)
- Advanced CRO for scale-stage brands
- International tax deep-dives by jurisdiction
- Paid ads mastery modules beyond first campaign setup

## Success Metrics (v1)
- Build completion within 3–4 weeks
- First 10 sales from organic channels
- <5% refund rate in first 30 days
- Qualitative feedback confirms reduced overwhelm and improved execution speed

## Risks and Mitigations
- **Risk:** Content becomes outdated as Shopify UI changes
  - **Mitigation:** Separate stable principles from update notes; schedule monthly patch reviews
- **Risk:** Product becomes too generic
  - **Mitigation:** Keep narrow ICP and include concrete decision rules and examples
- **Risk:** Low conversion from awareness channels
  - **Mitigation:** Publish “mistake teardown” posts that naturally lead to checklist CTA

## Phase 0–1 Execution Plan (Agentic Kickoff)
1. Lock brief assumptions and kill criteria
2. Compile source index from forums + Shopify docs + practitioner workflows
3. Extract recurring failure patterns into contradiction log
4. Freeze architecture (table of contents + asset map)

## Definition of Done (for initial development kickoff)
- Spec approved and stored in repository
- Brief files populated with buyer/problem/pricing/kill logic
- Workflow plan updated with milestone acceptance criteria
- Status file reflects active phase tasks and blockers
