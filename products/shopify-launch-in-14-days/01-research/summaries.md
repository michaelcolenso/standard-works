# Summaries

## Summary 1 — Buyer language confirms sequencing pain
Across startup and ecommerce communities, first-time merchants repeatedly ask for strict task order (what to configure first, what to defer). The dominant failure pattern is over-investing in theme polish before completing conversion-critical setup.

## Summary 2 — The highest-risk misses are operational, not aesthetic
New store owners report launch blockers around payments, shipping profiles, policy pages, domain/email trust setup, and analytics tracking. These misses directly affect ability to transact and diagnose performance after launch.

## Summary 3 — Existing education is fragmented
Free content and docs explain *how* to do individual tasks but rarely provide a constrained 14-day execution map with dependencies. The unmet need is an operator playbook that says “do X now, ignore Y until later.”

## Summary 4 — Buyer spend exists at multiple price bands
Comparable learning alternatives cluster at:
- low-ticket templates/checklists
- mid-ticket courses
- high-ticket freelancer/agency implementation

This supports a value-positioned $47–$97 launch playbook as a practical middle ground for cost-sensitive first-time founders.

## Summary 5 — Differentiation must be operationally specific
To win against free content, the product must include concrete assets: launch-day tests, app decision matrix, conversion-block checklist, and first-week optimization workflow rather than generic tutorial prose.
