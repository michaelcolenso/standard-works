# Insights

## High-confidence insights
1. **Order of operations is the product.**
   Buyer value comes from deciding *what not to do yet* as much as what to do now.
2. **Trust and transaction readiness drive first-launch outcomes.**
   Critical path: offer clarity → product pages → policies → payments/shipping/tax → checkout QA.
3. **Assetized guidance outperforms long-form theory for this buyer.**
   Checklists, pass/fail tests, and decision trees reduce overwhelm faster than conceptual education.
4. **Pricing headroom exists if time saved is explicit.**
   Positioning should quantify avoided wasted weeks and avoided rework.

## Product implications
- Create a strict 14-day roadmap with daily deliverables and completion criteria.
- Include “must-have vs nice-to-have” framework in every major setup category.
- Add explicit red-flag warnings tied to common first-store mistakes.
- Separate launch baseline from growth-stage optimizations to control scope creep.
