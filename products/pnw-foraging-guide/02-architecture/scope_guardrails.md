# Scope Guardrails — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

These rules are binding for v1.0. Any scope expansion requires an explicit decision log entry and must not delay the initial launch.

## Hard IN (must ship in v1.0)

- 50 species profiles (35 fungi + 15 plants), PNW-specific, organized by season + habitat
- Binary 3-step ID gate per species profile
- Look-alike comparison for every species with a dangerous look-alike (minimum 30 of 50 species)
- Harvest Log (Excel + Google Sheets dual format), pre-built and documented
- Pre-Harvest Safety Checklist (1-page printable)
- PNW Seasonal Calendar (all 50 species, 12 months, 3 elevation bands)
- Recipe & Preservation Quick-Ref (top 15 edible species)
- Poisonous species never-eat gallery (8 species minimum)
- Safety disclaimer language (reviewed against published guide standards)
- Quick Start instructions (user operational within 10 minutes of download)

## Hard OUT (excluded from v1.0; log requests for v2)

- Species outside WA, OR, northern ID, and BC Lower Mainland (no national scope creep)
- More than 50 species profiles (accuracy degrades; scope explodes production time)
- Medicinal use protocols (separate product category; different buyer + liability profile)
- Video content, QR codes, or internet-dependent features
- Notion as a primary deliverable format (online-only; violates offline-first constraint)
- Mycological cultivation / growing content (different buyer; different product)
- Recipes beyond the Quick-Ref card (recipe book is a separate future product)
- App development of any kind

## Size guardrails

- Core Guide PDF: 55–65 pages. If outline expands past 65 pages, cut species from Section 4 first.
- Harvest Log: maximum 4 sheets. If more tabs are proposed, evaluate whether they replace an existing tab rather than adding one.
- Safety Checklist: 1 page maximum. If it exceeds 1 page, the logic is too complex to be a field tool.

## Decision rules for scope disputes

**If a new feature is proposed during drafting:**
If it is in the Hard OUT list → reject without discussion.
If it is not in either list → apply the offline-first test: "Does this work without cell signal?" If NO → defer to v2. If YES → evaluate whether it fits within page/sheet guardrails without displacing a Hard IN item.

**If a species profile exceeds 1 page:**
Cut the prep note. If still over 1 page, cut the sustainability rule to one sentence. If still over 1 page, cut that species and replace with the next on the frequency ranking.
