# Product Promise — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

## One-sentence promise

After using this bundle, a PNW forager can confirm the safety of a specimen in the field without cell signal, log the find in under 3 minutes, and pull up every prior visit to that location next season.

## Expanded promise (for landing copy)

The PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System gives you:
- A 60-page offline-ready PDF covering the 50 most frequently encountered PNW wild edibles (35 fungi, 15 plants), each with a binary 3-step ID gate, look-alike comparison, and sustainability rule
- A pre-built Harvest Log (Excel + Google Sheets) with spot tracker, species log, and harvest volume columns — ready to use in 5 minutes, no setup required
- A PNW Seasonal Calendar showing all 50 species by month and elevation band
- A Pre-Harvest Safety Checklist (printable) for field use without signal
- A Quick-Reference Recipe & Preservation card for the top 15 edible species

What you stop doing: relying on memory for spot timing, posting photos to Reddit and waiting 24 hours for an ID, and rebuilding your tracking spreadsheet from scratch every season.

## What this does NOT promise

- This bundle does not replace mentorship from an experienced forager for first-time ID of any species. The decision trees confirm your field assessment; they do not substitute for hands-on learning.
- This bundle does not cover species outside the PNW region. Do not apply PNW season windows to other regions.
- This bundle does not provide medical advice. If you suspect poisoning, call Poison Control (1-800-222-1222) immediately.

## Done-when criteria (for production team)

The product is complete when a user can:
1. Open the PDF, navigate to any of the 50 species by habitat/season index, and answer all 3 ID gate questions using only what they can observe in the field — in under 5 minutes.
2. Open the Harvest Log spreadsheet and enter a new find (species, location, date, elevation, harvest weight) in under 3 minutes with no prior instruction beyond the Quick Start card.
3. Open the Seasonal Calendar and identify which 3 species are most likely in season this week at their elevation — in under 2 minutes.
