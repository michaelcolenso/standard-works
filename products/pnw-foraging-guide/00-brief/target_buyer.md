# Target Buyer — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

## Primary buyer

**The Serious PNW Weekend Forager** — 28–55 years old, based in the Seattle/Portland metro corridor or adjacent rural areas (western WA, western OR, BC Lower Mainland). Goes out 2–4 times per month during the 6–8 month active season (March–October). Owns a car, hikes moderate terrain, and already knows 5–15 species by name. Has been foraging 1–5 years — past the complete beginner stage but lacking a systematic ID methodology and no organized field records. Buys field guides and niche hobby products online. Active on r/foraging, r/mycology, r/PNWforaging, and local Facebook groups (Puget Sound Mycological Society, Oregon Mycological Society).

**Secondary buyers:**
- Subsistence foragers and farmers-market vendors selling chanterelles, nettles, and fiddleheads — need harvest volume logs and spot confidentiality tools
- Nature educators and mycology club organizers running beginner walks — need shareable species cards and safety checklists
- Gift-buyers purchasing for an active forager in their life (seasonally elevated demand, October–December)

## Top pains (ranked by urgency)

1. **Safety anxiety.** "I found something that looks like a chanterelle but I'm not 100% sure." Binary go/no-go identification keys don't exist in offline-ready formats. This is the primary purchase trigger — fear of poisoning themselves or family.
2. **Spot and timing loss.** "I found an amazing chanterelle patch last fall and now I can't remember the exact trail junction or when exactly they peaked." No tool integrates GPS coordinates, elevation, peak date, and harvest volume in one place.
3. **Knowledge fragmentation.** Notes are spread across phone photos, paper scraps, and memory. There is no searchable personal species log that travels offline.
4. **Season planning failure.** No tool shows all PNW species on a single elevation-tiered calendar, so foragers miss species outside their usual 3–4 targets.
5. **Recipe dead-ends.** Identified a species and harvested it, then had no integrated reference for preparation, drying, or preservation.

## Not for

- Complete beginners with zero prior foraging experience (safety risk; this product is a field decision tool, not a foraging curriculum from scratch)
- Foragers outside the PNW region (species profiles, season windows, and habitat references are PNW-specific; applying them elsewhere is misleading and dangerous)
- Professional mycologists or academic researchers (this is a practitioner field tool, not a taxonomic reference)
- Buyers looking for a standalone recipe book (recipes are supplementary, not the core deliverable)
