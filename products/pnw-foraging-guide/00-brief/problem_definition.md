# Problem Definition — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

## Buyer job-to-be-done

When I go out to forage wild foods or hunt mushrooms in the Pacific Northwest, I need a reliable identification + record-keeping system so I can harvest safely, remember productive spots across seasons, and stop losing hard-won knowledge to scattered notes and failing memory.

## Pain & stakes

- **Life-threatening misidentification.** Death Cap (Amanita phalloides) and Destroying Angel (Amanita bisporigera) are fatal within 24–72 hours of ingestion. Look-alike confusion is the #1 cause of fatal mushroom poisoning in the US. Existing free resources present identification criteria in narrative prose rather than binary decision trees, leaving a dangerous gap at the exact moment a field decision must be made without cell signal.
- **Spot amnesia.** Active foragers report revisiting promising terrain 2–3 weeks too early or too late because they have no season-indexed record of prior finds. Lost timing = lost harvest; for subsistence foragers and farmers-market sellers, that means lost income.
- **Data fragmentation.** Active foragers average 3–5 disconnected tools (paper notes, Google Sheets, photo roll, AllTrails, Facebook group posts) with no unified schema. Cross-referencing fails in the field when there is no cell signal.
- **No offline decision support.** Phone-based apps (iNaturalist, Shroomify) require signal to post for community ID confirmation and responses take hours to days — too slow for a same-day harvest decision. AI accuracy on ambiguous specimens is under 70% without expert verification.
- **Tight harvest windows.** PNW species windows are narrow and elevation-dependent: Chanterelles mid-July–October, Morels March–May at 2,000–4,000 ft, King Boletes September–October. Missing by 10 days yields zero harvest. No existing tool integrates elevation-zoned season calendars with personal spot-tracking history.

## Alternatives & failures

| Alternative | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| Arora's *All That the Rain Promises* (1991) | National scope; no PNW-specific look-alike comparison tables; no log system; no offline-ready digital format |
| iNaturalist / Shroomify apps | Requires cell signal; AI accuracy under 70% on ambiguous specimens; no harvest log or spot tracker |
| Generic Google Sheets foraging logs | No species reference integrated; user must build schema from scratch; no safety gate or binary decision tree |
| Reddit r/foraging ID requests | 24–72 hour response time; inconsistent expert presence; no searchable log per user |
| Etsy printable foraging journals | Aesthetic only — no decision logic, no look-alike comparisons, no species database integration |
| PNW Mushrooms website (online) | Requires signal; no log system; no offline-ready format; ID guidance is narrative prose, not binary |
