# Kill Criteria — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

Kill after two full brief+research loops if ANY of the following are true:

## Standard kill gates (from Playbook)
- No spending proof: cannot find 3+ instances of buyers paying real dollars for comparable foraging/mycology information products
- No differentiation: cannot articulate a concrete reason this bundle wins over Arora + a free Google Sheet
- Buyer unreachable organically: r/foraging, r/mycology, and local Facebook groups become inaccessible or hostile to product posts
- Cannot ship superior asset pack: 60-page PDF + 5 integrated templates cannot be produced to a higher standard than Arora or current Etsy listings within the 2–4 week creation window

## Product-specific kill triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Free sample zero-traction | Under 200 downloads in 14 days across r/foraging + r/mycology | Pause paid launch; rewrite positioning; run second free test |
| Safety accuracy failure | Any species profile fails adversarial review on look-alike criteria | Do not ship; rebuild affected profiles before any launch |
| Template unusability | Harvest log requires more than 10 minutes to set up for a new user | Simplify schema; retest |
| Legal/liability signal | Legal counsel flags unacceptable liability exposure from field identification advice | Add mandatory disclaimer language; reframe as reference supplement, not sole ID authority |
| Market saturation signal | A comparable PNW-specific PDF + log bundle launches at under $15 during production | Reassess differentiation; accelerate to beat to market or kill |

## Pivot options before kill

If the product stalls, attempt ONE of these pivots before killing:
1. **Narrow to mushrooms only** — drop plant foraging; market to r/mycology exclusively
2. **Toolkit-first** — launch the 5-template log system alone at $9, test conversion, then upsell the field guide
3. **Subscription model** — annual $19 update subscription for species status changes and new harvest windows, rather than one-time sale
