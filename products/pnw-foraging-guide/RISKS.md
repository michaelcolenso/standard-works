# Risks — pnw-foraging-guide

## Risk 1: Safety accuracy failure (Severity: CRITICAL)

**Risk:** A species profile contains an error in the ID gate or look-alike comparison that causes a buyer to misidentify a toxic species as edible.
**Probability:** Low if adversarial review is thorough; high if adversarial review is skipped.
**Mitigation:** Every species with a deadly look-alike (minimum 12 species) receives adversarial review against 2 independent published sources (Trudell/Ammirati + NAMA database) before shipping. A reviewer running through each ID gate as a naive user must fail at least once if the specimen described is a toxic look-alike. Do not ship any profile that fails this test.
**Refund/legal implication:** A poisoning event traced to an error in this guide would result in liability exposure beyond a refund. Disclaimer language is necessary but not sufficient — accuracy is the primary defense.
**Gate:** No profile ships with a known accuracy error regardless of timeline pressure.

## Risk 2: Reddit community backlash (Severity: MODERATE)

**Risk:** Free sample post is perceived as spam or low-effort promotion; removed by moderators or downvoted heavily; damages brand before paid launch.
**Probability:** Moderate — Reddit foraging communities are skeptical of commercial content.
**Mitigation:** Free sample must be genuinely high-quality (5 complete profiles, not previews); posted from an established account with prior community participation; no price mention in the post body; Gumroad link only in the comments with explicit "pay what you want / free" framing.
**If it happens:** Do not repost. Switch to Facebook groups (PSMS, Oregon Mushroom Hunters) and direct outreach to mycology club email lists.

## Risk 3: Etsy SEO invisibility (Severity: MODERATE)

**Risk:** Listing does not rank for relevant searches; organic traffic is zero.
**Probability:** Moderate — new seller with no Etsy history.
**Mitigation:** Optimize listing title and tags with high-intent PNW-specific terms ("PNW mushroom identification guide," "Pacific Northwest foraging log," "chanterelle ID decision tree"). Seed 3–5 genuine reviews via free copies to PSMS club members. Do not buy reviews.
**Recovery:** If under 50 listing views in 30 days, audit tags against Etsy search trends and revise; consider $5/day Etsy Promoted Listings for 14-day test.

## Risk 4: Scope creep extends timeline past harvest season window (Severity: MODERATE)

**Risk:** Production extends past late July; product launches after the peak chanterelle/summer season begins, missing the highest-demand entry point.
**Probability:** Moderate if 50-species scope is not enforced strictly.
**Mitigation:** Hard scope guardrail at 50 species (see scope_guardrails.md). If drafting falls behind, cut Section 4 (Winter species) entirely and ship a 45-species guide on time rather than a 50-species guide late.
**Target:** Free sample drops no later than June 1; full product listing live by July 1.

## Risk 5: Template complexity causes abandonment (Severity: LOW–MODERATE)

**Risk:** Harvest Log template is too complex; users don't set it up; the log's value proposition collapses; refund requests cite "too complicated."
**Probability:** Low if Quick Start card is clear; moderate if setup requires more than 10 minutes.
**Mitigation:** Test Harvest Log setup with 2 non-forager testers. If either takes more than 10 minutes, simplify schema. Maximum 8 columns in the Find Log sheet for v1.0.
**Refund threshold:** Refund rate above 5% triggers immediate template usability review.
