# Contradictions — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

## Contradiction 1: "Buyers want more species" vs. "accuracy degrades past ~50"

Community requests ask for comprehensive guides (100+ species). Expert mycologists warn that a guide attempting 100+ species without regional photo verification will have accuracy problems that create safety risks. Trudell/Ammirati covers ~150 species and is the standard reference — but it is 336 pages, not field-portable.

**Resolution:** Cap the guide at 50 species, but sequence them by real-world encounter frequency in the PNW. Label the guide explicitly as "the 50 species you are most likely to encounter" rather than "comprehensive." Users who need more will buy Trudell/Ammirati (positioned as the companion reference, not a competitor).

## Contradiction 2: "Offline-first" vs. "Notion template" in the bundle

Notion requires an internet connection for full functionality. Including a Notion template in an offline-first product bundle creates a contradiction.

**Resolution:** The Excel/Google Sheets Harvest Log is the primary offline log. The Notion template is an optional enhancement for users who do have connectivity — labeled clearly as "connected users only." It is a bonus, not a core deliverable. If it creates confusion, cut it from the bundle and offer it as a separate free add-on.

## Contradiction 3: Etsy audience vs. r/foraging audience pricing expectations

Reddit foragers express skepticism of paid information ("why would I pay for something I can find free online?"). Etsy buyers are pre-qualified to spend on curated niche products. These are different audiences with different pricing thresholds.

**Resolution:** Do not launch paid product on Reddit first. Use Reddit only for the free sample drop to build trust and email list. Route buyers to Gumroad/Etsy for purchase. Do not post price prominently in Reddit threads — let the content quality speak; interested users will follow the link.

## Contradiction 4: Safety disclaimers vs. buyer confidence

Heavy liability disclaimers ("do not eat based on this guide alone; consult an expert") undermine the core value proposition (use this to make safe field decisions). Too many disclaimers erode trust in the product's utility.

**Resolution:** One prominent disclaimer at the front of the guide + one on each species profile page (one line, below the ID gate). Decision trees are framed as "confirmation tools" alongside, not replacements for, field experience. The language is: "This gate confirms your field assessment. It does not replace hands-on mentorship for first-time identification of any species."
