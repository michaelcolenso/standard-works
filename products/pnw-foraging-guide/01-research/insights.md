# Insights — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

## Insight 1: Safety is the purchase trigger, not the content

Buyers do not purchase foraging guides primarily because they want to learn more species. They purchase because they want to feel safe making field decisions alone. The implication: the binary safety decision tree is the hero asset — it must appear in preview pages, in free sample content, and in the Etsy listing thumbnail. If the preview shows a pretty layout but no decision logic, conversion will be low.

**Design implication:** Each species profile leads with a 3-step ID gate, not a description paragraph.

## Insight 2: Spot tracking is the stickiest feature for repeat buyers

Users who track spots will return to the product every season. Users who use it only for identification may not. The harvest log is not just a bonus asset — it is the mechanism for annual re-engagement and word-of-mouth ("I use this every single time I go out"). Seasonal prompts (email or PDF reminder page) drive ongoing use.

**Design implication:** The harvest log template must be the most polished and functional of the 5 assets. Consider a standalone $9 listing for it as a test before the full bundle launch.

## Insight 3: The "no cell signal" constraint is a product-defining constraint, not a feature

Every feature decision must be tested against: "Does this work offline?" If the answer is no, the feature does not belong in the core deliverable. This means:
- PDF over app
- Printable decision trees over QR-code-linked content
- Pre-filled species data over user-searchable database
- Laminated field card format as a future upsell, not core

**Design implication:** Core guide is a static PDF; log is an offline-first spreadsheet (Excel/Google Sheets pre-filled, not Airtable or Notion as primary).

## Insight 4: "50 species" is the right scope signal, not the right organizing principle

Users think in terms of "what am I most likely to find this weekend" — not alphabetical species lists. The guide structure should be organized by season + habitat (coast, lowland forest, mountain, urban edge), not by taxonomy. This maps directly to how foragers plan trips.

**Design implication:** Guide structure = four seasonal sections × habitat type; species profiles grouped within sections, not A–Z.

## Insight 5: Etsy buyers respond to social proof around safety

Top-selling Etsy foraging journals use phrases like "used by mycology clubs," "safety-first design," and "expert-reviewed" in their listings. This language converts better than "beautiful design" or "printable." The product needs an expert review pass (even a single PSMS chapter advisor sign-off) to use equivalent language legitimately.

**Design implication:** Seek one PSMS advisor review before listing. Incorporate their name or society name in product description if permitted.
