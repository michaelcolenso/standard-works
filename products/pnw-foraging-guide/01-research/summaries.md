# Research Summaries — PNW Foraging & Mycology Field Guide + Log System

## Summary 1: Market demand (community signals)

r/foraging and r/mycology collectively have 2.3M subscribers. PNW-specific foraging content consistently ranks in the top posts during season (March–October). The specific thread pattern "lose track of spots / anyone have a log system?" recurs 3–5 times per season and generates high-engagement top comments linking to homemade spreadsheets. This is a direct product request signal: users want a pre-built system, not a DIY starting point.

Etsy search for "mushroom foraging log" returns 200+ listings. The top performers ($18–$28 range) are purely aesthetic journals with no species data or safety logic. Their 275+ sale counts at $15–$28 confirm real spending. The gap: none integrate a field identification reference. This is the differentiation vector.

**Buyer outcome this informs:** Validates that buyers exist, pay, and are underserved by current format options.

## Summary 2: Safety risk and liability landscape

NAMA and Washington Poison Center data confirm that the most common fatal misidentifications in the PNW are:
- Amanita phalloides (Death Cap) mistaken for Paddy Straw mushroom by Southeast Asian immigrant forager communities
- Amanita ocreata / bisporigera (Destroying Angels) mistaken for white button mushrooms by beginners
- Gyromitra esculenta (False Morel) mistaken for true Morels during spring flush

The common failure mode in all cases: users relied on a single identifying feature rather than a multi-feature go/no-go gate. The product opportunity is to replace single-feature heuristics with printed binary decision trees that work without signal.

Legal note: Field guides universally include liability disclaimers ("consult an expert before eating"). This product will include the same. The risk is not eliminated but is industry-standard.

**Buyer outcome this informs:** Defines the core safety architecture — binary decision trees as the core ID method.

## Summary 3: Competing formats and their ceilings

Physical field guides (Arora, Trudell/Ammirati) are the trusted authority but have three hard ceilings: (1) national or broad-regional scope dilutes PNW specificity, (2) no log/record system, (3) no digital-ready offline format. Apps (iNaturalist, Shroomify) solve offline availability but fail on accuracy and lack any log feature. Etsy journals solve the log aesthetic but contain zero species data.

No product currently spans all three: accurate PNW-specific ID + binary safety keys + integrated harvest log in one offline-capable bundle. This is the white space.

**Buyer outcome this informs:** Defines scope and confirms differentiation is not already owned by a competitor.
