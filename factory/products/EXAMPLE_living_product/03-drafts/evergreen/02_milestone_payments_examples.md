# Milestone Payments — Worked Example (Evergreen)

Outcome:
Use a payment schedule that preserves leverage and reduces disputes.

Steps:
1) Prefer milestones tied to verification.
2) Keep upfront payments small; ramp as deliverables become irreversible.
3) Require lien releases with payments (where applicable).

Example:
$250,000 project:
- 5% signing
- 10% after permit/buyout plan
- 15% foundation inspection passed
- 20% framing + rough inspections
- 20% insulation passed
- 20% substantial completion punchlist started
- 10% final completion + final lien releases

Failure modes:
- Front-loaded schedule (>10% upfront without documented procurement)
- Milestones defined as “progress” without evidence

Verification:
Each milestone maps to third-party verification (inspection pass or signed checklist + photos).
